Building from the Ground Up.

My journey into engineering didn't start with a textbook; it started with a curiosity to know exactly how things work. By the 7th grade, I was already managing network systems for schools like 'Elena Gjika' and 'Sami Frasher,' treating every technical challenge as a puzzle to be solved.

I've always believed that the best way to understand a system is to build it from scratch. That philosophy led me to design my own router, implement homelab versions of global internet infrastructure, and dive deep into cybersecurity—not just for the certification, but as a necessity to protect the experimental pipelines I host.

Today, my work lives at the intersection of IT Infrastructure, Machine Learning, and Mechanical Engineering. Whether I'm developing robotics algorithms, collecting urban la data, or refining a complex part in AutoCAD for 3D printing, I approach every project with the same goal: efficient, automated, and engineered from the foundation up.
